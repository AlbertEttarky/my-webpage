Fundamental Predictions

The Twin Antisymmetric Universes Theory (TAUT) offers a deterministic, unified framework for quantum physics and evolutionary biology. Its key predictions include:

  • Disappearance–Appearance Phenomenon (DAP): a continuous process replacing the quantum jump in atomic transitions.
  • Charged Atomic Entanglement (CAE): entanglement between electron–proton pairs in atoms, with biological analogs in humans.
  • Microscopic & macroscopic dualities, bridging matter–antimatter, space–time, and male–female principles.
  • Experimental proposals: atomic scattering experiments and DNA comparison tests between individuals.

Philosophical & Scientific Questions

TAUT addresses many profound questions, such as:

  • Why does the universe exhibit matter–antimatter asymmetry?
  • What is the meaning of death in physical terms?
  • How can deterministic laws describe the evolution of humans and particles alike?
  • Is it possible to build an Einstein–Rosen bridge between universes?
